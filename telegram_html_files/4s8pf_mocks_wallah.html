Select the sentence which is the correct Passive Voice version of the following sentence:-

"Some one has broken two of my dinner plates."

Two of my dinner plates are broken.
Two of my dinner plates are being broken by someone.
Two of my dinner plates had been broken.
Two of my dinner plates have been broken.
Solution

The correct answer is Option(4).

Key Points

  • The given sentence is in the active voice and uses the present perfect tense.
  • The subject of the sentence is "Someone" (though not explicitly mentioned, implied), and the object is "two of my dinner plates."
  • "two of my dinner plates." plural. Hence plural helping verb will be used.
  • The type of the sentence is declarative and indicates an action that has been completed in the recent past but its effect is still present.
  • For converting from active to passive voice in the present perfect tense, the format used is:
    • Active Format: Subject + has/have + V3 + Object
    • Passive Format: Object (Subjective case) + has/have been + V3 + by + Subject (Objective case)
  • Example: "They have completed the report." (Active) becomes "The report has been completed by them." (Passive)

Therefore, the correct answer is- “Two of my dinner plates have been broken.”

Additional Information

  • When changing from active to passive voice (or vice versa):
    • The tense of the sentence should not be changed.
    • Only the struc ture is altered to shift focus between the doer and the receiver of the action.
