Could you take me to the hospital today?

  • Generally in order to express ‘polite request’, the modal ‘Could’ is used.
  • In Passive voice:-
    • ‘Modal - Passive Voice (Present Tense)’, we always use ‘Modal + be’ as a helping verb and ‘Verb 3rd form (past participle)’ as a main verb.
    • Start the sentence with the modal “Could” as it’s an interrogative and here "the receiver of the action (Object) is ‘me” that will be changed into “I”.
    • Remember to use the preposition ”by” to introduce the doer of the action (Subject). Here in the sentence, the doer (subject) is ‘you’.
According to the above instructions, the final sentence would be ‘Could I be taken to the hospital by you today?’
 
 Additional Information

  • Interrogative Sentence - [Modal + object + be + verb 3rd form + subject?]

    • Could you switch on the light?

The girl said to me, “Where have you gone?”

The girl asked me where I have gone.
The girl asked me where I had been gone.
The girl asked me whether I had gone.
The girl asked me where I had gone.
Solution

The correct answer is 'The girl asked me where I had gone.'
Key Points

  • An interrogative sentence has a question.
  • The rule for changing Direct Speech to Indirect Speech:
    • The given sentence is in Present Perfect Tense.
    • Present Perfect Tense changes to Past Perfect tense.
    • Remove the commas and inverted commas.
    • Do not use that in the sentence of indirect speech.
    • The reporting verb said/said to is changed in asked, demanded, ordered, enquired as per the nature of the sentence.
    • Remove the question mark in the sentence of Indirect speech.
    • There are some questions that cannot be answered using 'YES/NO.'
      • To make the indirect speech of such questions, 'if/whether' will not be used.
      • The question is changed to assertive form and is simply placed after the reporting verb.
        • Example: The boy said to Ram, “Where is your office?” (Direct speech)
                           The boy asked Ram where his office was. (Indirect speech)
  • Therefore, the correct answer is 'The girl asked me where I had gone.'


Additional Information

  • If the sentence of Direct speech has a question that cannot be answered by saying yes or no then it does not uses “if” or “whether” in its Indirect speech.
    • Example:  She said to me, “Where did you buy this dress from?” (Direct speech)
                        She asked me where I had bought that dress from. (Indirect speech)

 

  • Interrogative वाक्य में एक प्रश्न होता है।
  • Direct Speech को Indirect Speech में बदलने का नियम:
    • दिया गया वाक्य Present Perfect Tense में है।
    • Present Perfect Tense, Past Perfect Tense में बदलता है।
    • Commas और inverted commas हटा दे।
    • Indirect speech के वाक्य में that प्रयोग न करें।
    • Said/said to रिपोर्टिंग verb वाक्य की प्रकृति के अनुसार asked, demanded, ordered, enquired में बदल जाती है।
    • Indirect speech के वाक्य में प्रश्न चिह्न हटा दें।
    • कुछ ऐसे प्रश्न हैं जिनका उत्तर 'YES/NO' में नहीं दिया जा सकता है।
      • ऐसे प्रश्नों को Indirect speech देने के लिए 'if/whether' का प्रयोग नहीं किया जाएगा।
      • Question को assertive form में बदल दिया जाता है और केवल reporting verb के बाद रखा जाता है।
        • Example: The boy said to Ram, “Where is your office?” (Direct speech)
